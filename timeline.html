Timeline

1999

Mayday was formed in 1999 when Ashin, Monster, Stone, Masa, and Guan You were studying at The Affiliated Senior High School of National Taiwan Normal University. The members of Mayday were childhood friends and came from the same elementary school. They were initially called So Band, but after discovering a band in Japan with the same name, they changed the name to Mayday, which means "help" in Chinese. The band performed in pubs and released an album in 2000.

2000

Mayday released their first album, Mayday's First Album, in 2000. The album was well-received and the band was nominated for Best Band at the 11th Golden Melody Awards. The band also held their first concert at the Taipei Municipal Stadium, which was attended by 20,000 fans.

2001

Mayday released their second album, Viva Love, in 2001. The album was a commercial success and the band won the Best Band award at the 12th Golden Melody Awards. The band also held their first concert in Hong Kong, which was attended by 10,000 fans.

2002

Mayday released their third album, People Life, Ocean Wild, in 2002. The album was a critical and commercial success and the band won the Best Band award at the 13th Golden Melody Awards. The band also held their first concert in Singapore, which was attended by 5,000 fans.

2003

Mayday released their fourth album, Time Machine, in 2003. The album was a commercial success and the band won the Best Band award at the 14th Golden Melody Awards. The band also held their first concert in Malaysia, which was attended by 8,000 fans.

2004

Mayday released their fifth album, 5th Mayday, in 2004. The album was a critical and commercial success and the band won the Best Band award at the 15th Golden Melody Awards. The band also held their first concert in China, which was attended by 15,000 fans.

2005

Mayday released their sixth album, Born to Love, in 2005. The album was a commercial success and the band won the Best Band award at the 16th Golden Melody Awards. The band also held their first concert in Japan, which was attended by 3,000 fans.
